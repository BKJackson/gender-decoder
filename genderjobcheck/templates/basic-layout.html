This tool uses the original list of gender-coded words from the research paper written by Danielle Gaucher, Justin Friesen, and Aaron C. Kay: Evidence That Gendered Wording in Job Advertisements Exists and Sustains Gender Inequality (Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, July 2011, Vol 101(1), p109-28).

The code is on GitHub, and I'd welcome any suggestions about the wordlists it uses, by pull request or tweet.

Thanks to Stephen Shearman whose blog post introduced me to the research last year; and to Made by Many's Isaac Pinnock whose recent blog post gave me the final nudge towards building this.
